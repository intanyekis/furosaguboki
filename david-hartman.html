Biography

David Hartman was born in Rhode Island on May 19, 1935. Journalist and media personality who first became famous as an actor on television shows like The Bold Ones: The New Doctors, The Virginian, and Ironside. He later served as the co-host of ABC’s Good Morning America from 1975 to 1987 and hosted a variety of documentaries for PBS and the Discovery Channel. He co-hosted Good Morning America with Nancy Dussault from 1975 to 1977. He was a standout baseball player at Mount Hermon School but turned a baseball scholarship to attend Duke University. He served in the U.S. Air Force for three years.
