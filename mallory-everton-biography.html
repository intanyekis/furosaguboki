Who is Mallory Everton?

Mallory Everton is an American actress, comedian, and writer.

She is known as a cast member of the Brigham Young University television sketch comedy program, Studio C. The program has 2 million subscribers and more than 1.4+ billion views on Youtube.

Mallory Everton’s Early life-Ancestry

Mallory Everton was born on September 20, 1989, in Portland, Oregon, to Bob and Colleen Everton. She has an American ancestry.

She is the youngest among her six siblings; three older brothers, Travis, Zach, and Beau, and two older sisters, Hailey and Melissa.

She is a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

In which school did Mallory Everton attend?

Mallory Everton did her schooling at Liberty High School in Hillsboro, Oregon. During her school days, she was the valedictorian of her class.

After she graduated, she moved to Utah to attend Brigham Young University, Provo, Utah.

Her original plan was to pursue a medical degree, therefore, she enrolled in the university as a pre-med. However, she later changed her major and began studying film studies instead.

In 2012, she graduated with a bachelor’s degree in Media Arts Studies with an emphasis in screenwriting, editing, and directing.

That year, Mallory Everton also received a scholarship from the Ray and Tye Noorda Foundation, Lindon, Utah, US for her exceptional work in media arts fiction.

Mallory Everton-Career

TV series

Mallory Everton made her TV debut in 2012, in an episode of ‘Pretty Darn Funny’.

She saw her best friend Whitney Call’s audition for Divine Comedy, she also gave a successful audition and became a cast member.

In 2012, Matt Reese and Jared Shores created ‘Studio C’. Many of their colleagues in the ‘Divine Comedy’, including Everton, joined them.

Sketch comedy, Others

Some of Mallory Everton’s most popular sketches are ‘The Smiths, ‘Worst…Ever’, and ‘Bad Karma’.

Mallory Everton also works as the director and writer on the show.

Movie

Mallory Everton made her silver screen debut in the 2017 American comedy film, We Love You, Sally Carmichael!, starring Felicia Day, Elizabeth Tulloch, Christopher Gorham, and others.

The film tells the story of Simon Hayes, a bitter, serious novelist, who uses the pseudonym Sally Carmichael to write a series of romance novels about a merman and a human girl.
